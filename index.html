Introduction

In recent years, the intersection of healthcare and technology has gained significant momentum. As global longevity continues to rise, societies are faced with a compelling challenge: while people are living longer, the years added to their lives are not necessarily healthy ones. According to the World Health Organization, while Canadians now enjoy an average lifespan of 82.8 years, an impressive surge from the 1960s, their healthspan—the period in one’s life devoid of chronic illnesses or debilitating conditions—ends over a decade earlier. This means that many elderly individuals spend a substantial part of their life coping with health and social challenges.

The Covid-19 pandemic has further accentuated this issue. As the virus spread rapidly, elderly individuals, particularly those in nursing homes or living alone, faced heightened risks, leading to recommended or enforced isolation. Such isolation, though critical for physical safety, often inadvertently deteriorated mental well-being, triggering feelings of loneliness and disconnect.

In this evolving landscape, technology offers a beacon of hope. Mobile applications and pervasive computing can bridge the gap, offering tools and platforms that not only connect the elderly to their loved ones but also monitor and enhance their physical and mental well-being. However, the adoption rate of such technologies among the elderly remains suboptimal, primarily due to design issues. A significant portion of technology solutions are not tailored to the elderly's unique needs, taking into account their potential physical, cognitive, or technological limitations.

Thus, the primary goal of this project is to design an application that holistically addresses these challenges. The app aims to foster a sense of connection and provide tools to promote physical and mental health for the elderly, especially during times of social distancing or self-quarantine. By placing the unique needs and preferences of the elderly at the forefront of the design process, this app will strive to offer an elder-friendly interface that is intuitive, accessible, and most importantly, beneficial.

Conlusion

Lessons, Challenges, and Forward-Looking Perspectives

Our endeavor to create an app tailored for the elderly provided profound insights. We faced challenges in ensuring accessibility and intuitive design, recognizing the unique needs of our target audience. Nevertheless, these challenges elucidated the importance of inclusivity in technology. As we look to the future, vast opportunities arise to refine and expand our app, ensuring technology remains a boon, not a barrier, for our aging population.
