Research & Analysis

Scientific Literature Review

In delving into the depths of the current scientific literature, it becomes unequivocally clear that elderly individuals are grappling with pressing challenges, paramount among them being social isolation. Various studies elucidate the stark reality: a significant portion of the elderly population finds itself ensnared in the throes of isolation, a situation exacerbated by factors such as living alone, loss of loved ones, or physical immobility. Recent statistics reveal that a staggering 34% of seniors are experiencing social isolation.

The ramifications of such isolation are not merely emotional; they manifest physically and can be dire. Social isolation has been linked to an array of health risks, ranging from increased high blood pressure, heart disease, obesity, weakened immune function, anxiety, depression, cognitive decline, dementia, alzheimer’s disease, and death.

Moreover, the health challenges for the elderly are not limited to the consequences of isolation. Chronic diseases, an unwelcome companion of old age, further compound their daily struggles. A staggering 95% of elderly individuals suffer from at least one chronic disease, requiring continuous monitoring, medication, and adjustments to their lifestyle. Such chronic ailments not only inflict physical discomfort but also cast shadows of anxiety, depression, and uncertainty.

Physical activity is paramount for elderly individuals to maintain their health and well-being as shown by many studies. Engaging in regular exercise not only strengthens the cardiovascular and musculoskeletal systems, but it also improves balance, coordination, and flexibility, reducing the risk of falls and fractures. Moreover, physical activity has been linked to enhanced cognitive function, mood elevation, and a decrease in the symptoms of many chronic diseases common in older age. By staying active, the elderly can enjoy a more robust quality of life, prolonged independence, and a potential increase in their healthspan, making it an essential component of healthy aging.

Recognizing these multifaceted challenges, our research endeavors to pave a path forward. It seeks to explore how technology, specifically mobile applications, can be a lifeline for the elderly—connecting them, supporting them, and enhancing their quality of life in the face of social isolation and chronic diseases.

Building on the insights gleaned from the extensive research presented above, it's evident that addressing the multifaceted challenges faced by the elderly requires a comprehensive and integrated solution. In response, our preliminary proposal is the design of a user-friendly mobile application that seamlessly melds three pivotal domains, each targeting a unique facet of elderly well-being:

1) Social Media Integration: To combat the pervasive issue of social isolation, our app will offer features that connect directly to popular social media platforms like Facebook. By doing so, it will enable seniors to effortlessly stay in touch with family, friends, and communities, ensuring they always remain a touch away from meaningful interactions.

2) Health Management Tools: Recognizing the prevalence of chronic diseases among the elderly, our app will provide centralized planning and notifications for crucial aspects of health management. This includes reminders for medical appointments and medication intake, streamlining their healthcare routines. With direct links to platforms like Bonjour Santé, seniors will have a consolidated view of their health-related activities an commitments.

3) Physical Health Promotion: Ensuring that seniors remain physically active is integral to their holistic well-being. Our app will therefore integrate functionalities from platforms like Google Fit, providing seniors with personalized fitness plans, tracking tools, and progress insights. This will encourage and guide them in maintaining a regular fitness regimen, tailored to their specific needs and capabilities.

However, while our preliminary proposal is grounded in scientific literature and existing data, it's crucial to validate these insights directly from the primary stakeholders – the elderly population. To ensure our app is not just functional but also resonates with the needs and preferences of its users, we intend to conduct a series of surveys and interviews. Through these firsthand interactions, we aim to gather direct opinions and feedback, helping us fine-tune our approach and ensuring the app serves as a genuine aid to the elderly in their pursuit of a better quality of life.

Survey Results

The review of the scientific literature provided us with a deeper insight into the issue. Based on this, we created a survey via Google Forms to gather the elderly population's perspectives with a sample of 6 elderly people.



Key Insights

Centralized App Preference

All respondents prefer having an app that centralizes many features (like medication reminders, social connection, and news) instead of using multiple apps. This suggests a strong demand for all-in-one applications.

Hesitations for App Usage

  • The most substantial concern is that users find some apps confusing or not intuitive (54.5%).
  • Concerns about privacy and data follow at 27.3%.
  • 18.2% of users are deterred from downloading more apps due to storage issues or slowed performance on their devices.

Characteristics of a Good App

  • A dominant 60% of users believe that a simple and easy-to-understand design is crucial for an app.
  • 30% value useful features that cater to their needs.
  • Only 10% emphasize the importance of good customer support or help.

Data Privacy

All respondents value the privacy of their data when using an app. This is in line with the earlier mentioned concern about privacy and data.

Seeking Tech Help:

  • 50% frequently seek assistance from friends, family, or online sources when they face challenges with tech or apps.
  • 33.3% do so rarely, and 16.7% never seek help.

Recommendations

Based on our survey results, we have identified a notable interest in news that was not seen in our scientific literature review. Therefore, we will integrate news features into our app.

From the scientific literature review and the survey, we have decided to design an all-in-one app that integrates 1) Social Media, 2) Health & Fitness, and 3) News. Here are our recommendations:

  • Centralized App Development: Given the overwhelming preference for an all-in-one app, we should prioritize creating a centralized application that offers multiple features. This reduces the need for users to switch between different apps, making their experience more seamless.
  • User-Friendly Design: Since a significant portion of users find some apps confusing, it's essential for the centralized app to have a simple, intuitive, and easy-to-navigate design. This will cater to the 60% of users who prioritize this feature.
  • Prioritize Privacy: Considering the concerns about data privacy and the importance users place on this, the app should have robust data protection measures in place. Transparent policies regarding data usage, storage, and sharing should be clearly communicated to the users.


User Personas & Journeys

To further refine our application and ensure it aligns with the specific needs and challenges of our target audience, we've developed detailed user personas. These personas, grounded in our research findings, provide insights into potential user behaviors, preferences, and challenges. Along with each persona, we have mapped out a user journey that traces their interactions with the app from initial discovery to potential advocacy.

1. Marguerite Beauchamp, The Active Retiree:

A spirited 70-year-old former school teacher, Marguerite is always eager to learn and adapt. Although she's comfortable using her smartphone for basic tasks, her minor arthritis sometimes makes touchscreens a challenge. Marguerite yearns to stay mentally and physically active indoors, wishes to maintain connections with her friends and former students, and seeks user-friendly digital tools to facilitate this. She appreciates straightforward app designs and often remarks, "I love learning new things, especially on my phone. But I need it to be straightforward."

2. Bernard Lefevre, The Homebound Elder:

At 85, Bernard, a former factory worker, primarily wishes to connect with his family, especially his beloved grandkids. He has recently ventured into the world of tablets, a gift from his grandchildren. With early signs of dementia and a hearing impairment, Bernard needs an application that offers reminders for his medication and doctor's appointments. He often mentions, "I just want to see my grandkids and hear their voices." while also expressing a desire not to become a burden due to his occasional forgetfulness.

3. Elaine Dubois, The Tech-Savvy Senior:

Elaine, 78, is a testament to the adage that age is just a number when it comes to technological prowess. A former IT specialist, she is adept at using her smartphone, desktop, and even smart home devices. Despite her limited mobility due to a hip injury, her cognitive abilities are as sharp as ever. Elaine seeks resources to get essentials delivered to her doorstep, as well as tools to monitor her physical health. She confidently states, "Technology has always been my ally, and age won't change that."

4. Jacques Tremblay, The Care Facility Resident:

At 90, Jacques, a former army officer, has witnessed the rapid evolution of technology throughout his lifetime. Although he finds modern gadgets a tad overwhelming, his spirit is willing to learn. Being wheelchair-bound, Jacques craves virtual experiences that connect him to the outside world. He wishes for activities he can enjoy with fellow residents or family, and a hassle-free method to communicate with the care facility staff. Reflecting his resilience, he often says, "The world has moved so fast, but I want to keep up, even if it's just a little."
